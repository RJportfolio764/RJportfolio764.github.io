Key Insights
The retention story behind the dashboard.
Insight 01

Overtime is a 2.9× attrition multiplier

30.5% of overtime employees leave, vs 10.4% of non-overtime — 416 employees currently sit in this high-risk bucket. The single strongest lever in the dataset.

Insight 02

Sales Representatives leave at 2.5× the company average

39.8% annual attrition for Sales Reps vs 16.1% company-wide. The risk is concentrated enough to target with a role-specific retention program.

Insight 03

Attrition is front-loaded in years 0-2

29.8% of employees in their first 2 years leave, vs just 8.1% of 10+ year veterans — a 3.7× gap. The leak is in year-one experience and onboarding, not long-tenure disengagement.

AI Agent Pipeline
The repeatable workflow behind the final dashboard.

I treated AI as a project team, not a single chat. Each agent had a narrow job, read only the right inputs, produced files for the next stage, and left an audit trail I could review before moving forward. The only manual touchpoint was a short Power BI Desktop finish to refresh, apply the theme, bind slicers, and export.

Research the domain and pick the right KPIs
Read the project brief, researched industry-standard HR attrition benchmarks, and recommended the four KPIs that matter most: overall attrition rate, overtime attrition, max-risk role, and early-tenure attrition. Flagged dataset risk columns for the profiler to watch.
